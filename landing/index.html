Built for occasional signers

Most people don't sign documents every day - yet the industry is built around recurring monthly fees designed for the rare case that they do.

You sign a SAFE for an investor. A lease. A contractor agreement. A consent form for school. Maybe a dozen times a year, if you're busy.

The mainstream platforms charge a monthly subscription regardless of usage - billing in full whether you signed one document or one hundred. Cancel and resubscribe each time something needs signing? That's friction the industry profits from.

FreeSign exists because the technology, legal frameworks, and infrastructure to sign documents are mature commodities. ESIGN Act, UETA, and eIDAS make digital signatures legally binding. The marginal cost of one signature is fractions of a cent. There is no reason this should require a recurring fee.

So we built a free, open-source alternative - no subscription, no upsells, no feature paywalls. Sign as many or as few documents as you need. The source is on GitHub: self-host it, audit it, or use this hosted instance at no charge.

Everything you actually need

Drag-and-drop signing

Upload a PDF, drop signature fields where they go, send to recipients. Same flow you're used to from DocuSign.

🔐

Legally binding

ESIGN Act compliant in the US, UETA across all 50 states, eIDAS in the EU. Real audit trail with each signature.

📜

Audit trail

Every action - viewed, signed, IP, timestamp - recorded and embedded in the final PDF for non-repudiation.
